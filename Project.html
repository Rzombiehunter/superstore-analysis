######################### Which regions generate the most sales and profit?
region_data = data.groupby('Region')[['Sales', 'Profit']].sum()

## Reset the index of region_data to create region_data_reset
region_data_reset = region_data.reset_index()

plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
sns.barplot(x='Region', y='Sales', data=region_data_reset, color='skyblue', label='Sales')
sns.barplot(x='Region', y='Profit', data=region_data_reset, color='lightgreen', label='Profit')
plt.title('Sales and Profit by Region', fontsize=16)
plt.xlabel('Region', fontsize=12)
plt.ylabel('Amount ($)', fontsize=12)
plt.legend()
plt.grid(axis='y')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
  1. The West Region Outperforms All Others Sales and profit are both highest in the West, suggesting:

Strong market demand

Efficient operations (possibly better shipping/logistics)

Product-market fit with high-margin items

The West is likely a strategic stronghold for this company.

  1. The East Region is Also a Strong Performer The East ranks second in both metrics.

This region presents a stable growth opportunity, especially if marketing or delivery channels are strengthened.

  1. The South is Middle-of-the-Road Moderate sales but lower profitability suggest:

Higher operational costs

Possibly heavier discounting or returns

Suboptimal product mixes (low-margin items selling more)

  1. The Central Region is Underperforming Lowest in both sales and profit, possibly even negative profit.

Potential causes:

High shipping or return costs

Poor market penetration

Excessive discounting or promotion of unprofitable products

Needs a targeted diagnostic: logistics, customer satisfaction, or localized pricing strategies might be failing.
